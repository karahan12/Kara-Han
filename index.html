ABOUT JAKARTA, INDONESIA

Jakarta (formerly known as Batavia or Djakarta) is Indonesia's massive capital and sits on the northwest coast of the island of Java. Jakarta itself has a city population of 10,770,487(2020) and Indonesia (the country) has a population of 264 million (2017). Geographically, Jakarta is situated on the northwest coast of Java, at the mouth of the Ciliwung River on Jakarta Bay, and inlet of the Java Sea and is part of the continent Asia. The climate is tropical and humid. The average mean temperatures are 26 degrees celsius in January and 28 degrees celsisus in October and the annual rainfall is more than 1,700mm. Jakarta has an area of 661.5km squared and the population density of 14,464/km squared. The main industries are electronics, automotive, chemical, mechanical engineering, and biomedical industries.
